# Research Frontiers in Automation

**Version**: 1.0 | **Date**: October 25, 2025

---

## 1. Safe RL for Physical Systems

**Challenge**: Autonomous systems operating in physical world must guarantee safety while learning. Certification remains open.

**Open questions**:
- How to certify learned controllers for arbitrary nonlinear systems?
- Can safety and optimality be balanced rigorously?
- Scalability to high-dimensional systems?

## 2. Robust Sim2Real Transfer

**Challenge**: Models trained in simulation often fail on real hardware due to visual/dynamics differences.

**Open questions**:
- How to characterize which randomizations transfer? (Theory lacking)
- Can we transfer to completely different robot morphologies?
- How to handle long-horizon tasks (100+ steps) with compounding errors?

## 3. Formal Verification + ML

**Challenge**: Neural networks lack interpretability; difficult to formally verify safety properties.

**Limitations**:
- Abstractions become very loose (overly conservative)
- High-dimensional systems intractable
- No complete solution for arbitrary architectures

## 4. Human-Robot Collaboration

**Challenge**: Robots increasingly share workspaces with humans; safety, usability, and productivity requirements create tension.

**Key research areas**:
- **Intuitive interfaces**: Gesture/speech control, tactile feedback
- **Intention prediction**: Infer human goals; adapt robot behavior
- **Safety certification**: Formal guarantees during physical interaction

## 5. Explainable Automation

**Challenge**: Black-box AI decisions lack transparency, hindering trust and accountability in critical systems.

**Open questions**:
- How to explain complex multi-step automation workflows?
- Can we trade interpretability for performance systematically?
- User studies: Do explanations actually increase human trust?

## 6. Edge AI Optimization

**Challenge**: Deploy large AI models on resource-constrained edge devices (mobile, IoT, robots).

**Techniques**:
- Quantization: INT8 models (vs FP32); 75% memory reduction, 4x speedup
- Pruning: Remove low-weight connections; 90%+ parameters can be pruned
- Knowledge distillation: Train small "student" model to mimic large "teacher"

## 7. Multi-Agent Coordination

**Challenge**: Coordinate 100s-1000s of robots (swarms, factories, power grids) without central control.

**Applications**:
- Warehouse automation: 15K+ robots in Amazon warehouses (centralized system); research focuses on decentralized
- Drone swarms: 100+ drones coordinating formation flight, search & rescue
- Power grid: Millions of prosumers (producers/consumers) balancing supply-demand

## 8. Autonomous Policy & Ethics

**Challenge**: As systems operate autonomously without human oversight, who makes decisions about ethical trade-offs?

**Key questions**:
- **Trolley problem variants**: In unavoidable collision, should autonomous vehicle minimize casualties or protect occupants?
- **Liability**: Who is responsible for autonomous system failures—manufacturer, deployer, or no one?
- **Transparency**: Should algorithms be transparent to public and regulators?
