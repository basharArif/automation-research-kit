# Annotated Bibliography

**Version**: 1.0 | **Date**: October 25, 2025

---

This document provides a curated list of key academic papers, books, and articles that form the theoretical and practical foundation of modern automation.

## Foundational Texts

1.  **Wiener, N. (1948). *Cybernetics: Or Control and Communication in the Animal and the Machine*. MIT Press.**
    *   **Annotation:** The foundational text of cybernetics, which established the principles of feedback, control, and communication as a unified field applicable to both machines and living organisms.

2.  **Åström, K. J., & Murray, R. M. (2021). *Feedback Systems: An Introduction for Scientists and Engineers*. Princeton University Press.**
    *   **Annotation:** The modern standard textbook on control theory. It provides a comprehensive introduction to the principles of feedback systems, including PID control, state-space models, and stability analysis.

3.  **Minorsky, N. (1922). Directional stability of automatically steered bodies. *Journal of the American Society of Naval Engineers*, 34(2), 280-309.**
    *   **Annotation:** The seminal paper that first formalized the three-term (Proportional-Integral-Derivative) controller, based on observations of human helmsmen. This is the origin of the PID controller.

## AI and Machine Learning

4.  **LeCun, Y., Bengio, Y., & Hinton, G. (2015). Deep learning. *Nature*, 521(7553), 436-444.**
    *   **Annotation:** A landmark paper by the "godfathers of AI" that provides a high-level overview of deep learning and its potential to revolutionize fields like computer vision, natural language processing, and robotics.

5.  **Sutton, R. S., & Barto, A. G. (2018). *Reinforcement learning: An introduction*. MIT press.**
    *   **Annotation:** The standard textbook on reinforcement learning, covering the theoretical foundations of the field, from Markov decision processes to deep reinforcement learning algorithms.

6.  **Krizhevsky, A., Sutskever, I., & Hinton, G. E. (2012). Imagenet classification with deep convolutional neural networks. In *Advances in neural information processing systems* (pp. 1097-1105).**
    *   **Annotation:** The paper that introduced AlexNet, the deep convolutional neural network that won the 2012 ImageNet competition and kickstarted the deep learning revolution.

## Robotics

7.  **Khatib, O. (1986). Real-time obstacle avoidance for manipulators and mobile robots. *The International Journal of Robotics Research*, 5(1), 90-98.**
    *   **Annotation:** A classic paper that introduced the artificial potential field method for robot motion planning, a technique that is still widely used today.

8.  **Quigley, M., Conley, K., Gerkey, B., Faust, J., Foote, T., Leibs, J., ... & Ng, A. Y. (2009). ROS: an open-source Robot Operating System. In *ICRA workshop on open source software* (Vol. 3, No. 3.2, p. 5).**
    *   **Annotation:** The paper that introduced the Robot Operating System (ROS), which has since become the de facto standard for robotics research and development.

## Safety and Ethics

9.  **Koopman, P. (2017). How safe is safe enough? A sociotechnical perspective. In *2017 IEEE International Symposium on Software Reliability Engineering Workshops (ISSREW)* (pp. 104-104). IEEE.**
    *   **Annotation:** A thought-provoking paper that explores the challenges of defining and achieving safety in complex sociotechnical systems, such as autonomous vehicles.

10. **Amodei, D., Olah, C., Steinhardt, J., Christiano, P., Schulman, J., & Mané, D. (2016). Concrete problems in AI safety. *arXiv preprint arXiv:1606.06565*.**
    *   **Annotation:** A paper that outlines a set of practical research problems in AI safety, including how to prevent AI systems from having negative side effects, and how to ensure that they are robust to distributional shift.
