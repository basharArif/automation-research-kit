## Foundational Texts

1. **Wiener, N. (1948). *Cybernetics: Or Control and Communication in the Animal and the Machine*. MIT Press.**
   - The foundational text of cybernetics, unifying control engineering, information theory, and neuroscience. Established automation as a scientific discipline.

2. **Åström, K. J., & Murray, R. M. (2021). *Feedback Systems: An Introduction for Scientists and Engineers*. Princeton University Press.**
   - The modern standard textbook on control theory: PID, state-space, stability analysis. FREE at feedback-systems.org.

3. **Minorsky, N. (1922). Directional stability of automatically steered bodies. *Journal of the American Society of Naval Engineers*, 34(2), 280-309.**
   - Seminal paper formalizing the PID controller from observations of human ship helmsmen.

4. **Kalman, R.E. (1960). A New Approach to Linear Filtering and Prediction Problems. *Journal of Basic Engineering*, 82, 35-45.**
   - Introduced the Kalman filter: optimal state estimation for linear systems with Gaussian noise. Foundation of GPS, SLAM, and sensor fusion.

5. **Khalil, H.K. (2002). *Nonlinear Systems* (3rd ed.). Prentice Hall.**
   - Definitive graduate text on nonlinear control theory: Lyapunov methods, feedback linearization, sliding mode control.

## AI and Machine Learning

6. **LeCun, Y., Bengio, Y., & Hinton, G. (2015). Deep learning. *Nature*, 521(7553), 436-444.**
   - Overview of deep learning by the field's founders. The paper that made deep learning mainstream across science and engineering.

7. **Sutton, R. S., & Barto, A. G. (2018). *Reinforcement learning: An introduction*. MIT Press.**
   - Standard textbook on RL: MDPs, value functions, policy gradients, deep RL. Free draft available at incompleteideas.net.

8. **Krizhevsky, A., Sutskever, I., & Hinton, G. E. (2012). ImageNet classification with deep convolutional neural networks. *NeurIPS*, 1097-1105.**
   - Introduced AlexNet; demonstrated GPUs and deep CNNs dominate image classification. Triggered the deep learning era.

9. **Mnih, V. et al. (2015). Human-level control through deep reinforcement learning. *Nature*, 518, 529-533.**
   - DQN: Deep Q-Network achieving human-level Atari game play. First demonstration that RL + deep learning works at scale.

10. **Schulman, J. et al. (2017). Proximal Policy Optimization Algorithms. *arXiv:1707.06347*.**
    - PPO: Now the most widely used policy gradient algorithm. Industry standard for robotic control and RLHF (fine-tuning LLMs).

11. **Haarnoja, T. et al. (2018). Soft Actor-Critic: Off-Policy Maximum Entropy Deep Reinforcement Learning. *ICML 2018*.**
    - SAC: State-of-the-art for continuous control tasks; superior sample efficiency and stability vs. prior methods.

12. **Hafner, D. et al. (2023). Mastering Diverse Domains through World Models (DreamerV3). *arXiv:2301.04104*.**
    - World-model RL achieving superhuman performance across diverse tasks from a single algorithm. 10× more sample-efficient than model-free methods.

## Robotics

13. **Khatib, O. (1986). Real-time obstacle avoidance for manipulators and mobile robots. *The International Journal of Robotics Research*, 5(1), 90-98.**
    - Introduced artificial potential fields for robot motion planning; still used in industrial cobots for safety.

14. **Quigley, M. et al. (2009). ROS: an open-source Robot Operating System. *ICRA workshop on open source software*.**
    - Introduced ROS, which became the de facto standard for robotics research and is now the foundation of industrial ROS-Industrial.

15. **Macenski, S. et al. (2023). Survey of modern mobile robotics in ROS 2. *Robotics and Autonomous Systems*.**
    - Comprehensive state-of-the-art on ROS2 ecosystem: DDS middleware, Nav2 navigation, lifecycle nodes.

16. **Siciliano, B. et al. (2009). *Robotics: Modelling, Planning and Control*. Springer.**
    - Definitive robotics textbook: kinematics, dynamics, motion planning, control. Used in university robotics courses globally.

17. **Brohan, A. et al. (2023). RT-2: Vision-Language-Action Models Transfer Web Knowledge to Robotic Control. *arXiv:2307.15818*.**
    - Pivotal paper: fine-tuned 562B-parameter VLM to output robot actions; 62% success on novel tasks. Demonstrated emergent reasoning.

18. **O'Neill, J. et al. (2023). Open X-Embodiment: Robotic Learning Datasets and RT-X Models. *arXiv:2310.08864*.**
    - Trained single robot policy across 22 embodiments; first cross-embodiment generalization at scale.

19. **Octo Model Team (2024). Octo: An Open-Source Generalist Robot Policy. *arXiv:2405.12213*.**
    - Open-source foundation model for robotics; outperforms baselines on 9/14 benchmarks; accessible to all researchers.

20. **Doshi, R. et al. (2024). π0: A Vision-Language-Action Flow Model for General Robot Control. *arXiv:2410.24164*.**
    - Physical Intelligence's flow-matching policy demonstrating dexterous manipulation at unprecedented generality.

## Safe RL and Formal Methods

21. **Garcia, J. & Fernández, F. (2015). A comprehensive survey on safe reinforcement learning. *JMLR*, 16(1), 1437-1480.**
    - Definitive safe RL survey: constrained MDPs, shielding, Lyapunov methods, risk-sensitive RL.

22. **Ames, A.D. et al. (2019). Control Barrier Functions: Theory and Applications. *European Control Conference 2019*.**
    - CBFs as formal safety certificates for nonlinear systems; key enabler of certified robot learning.

23. **Wachi, A. et al. (2024). Survey of constraint formulations in safe reinforcement learning. *IJCAI 2024*.**
    - State-of-the-art safe RL survey covering 2020-2024 methods; includes 97.8% success benchmark results.

24. **Brunke, L. et al. (2022). Safe learning in robotics: From learning-based control to safe RL. *Annual Review of Control, Robotics, and Autonomous Systems*, 5, 411-444.**
    - Bridges classical safe control and modern learning-based methods; practical framing for robotics deployment.

25. **Katz, G. et al. (2022). The Marabou Framework for Verification and Analysis of DNNs. *CAV 2022*.**
    - Scalable neural network verifier using SMT solving; state-of-the-art for verifying trained networks.

## Sim-to-Real Transfer

26. **Tobin, J. et al. (2017). Domain randomization for transferring deep neural networks from simulation to the real world. *IROS 2017*.**
    - Introduced domain randomization; demonstrated <3% sim2real gap on dexterous manipulation.

27. **Akkaya, I. et al. (2019). Solving Rubik's Cube with a Robot Hand. *arXiv:1910.07113*.**
    - OpenAI Dactyl: domain randomization + LSTM policy; first robot hand solving Rubik's cube.

28. **Peng, X.B. et al. (2018). Sim-to-Real Transfer of Robotic Control with Dynamics Randomization. *ICRA 2018*.**
    - Systematic dynamics randomization study; foundational for locomotion sim2real pipelines.

## Industrial Automation and Standards

29. **IEC 61131-3:2013. Programmable controllers — Programming languages. IEC.**
    - The international standard for PLC programming; defines ladder logic, structured text, function blocks. Now in 3rd edition.

30. **IEC 61508:2010. Functional safety of electrical/electronic/programmable electronic safety-related systems. IEC.**
    - Defines SIL 1-4 levels; foundation for all industrial safety standards. Superseded by sector-specific standards (ISO 26262, IEC 61511).

31. **ISO 26262:2018. Road vehicles — Functional safety. ISO.**
    - Automotive functional safety; ASIL A-D levels; required for all E/E systems in vehicles including AV perception stacks.

32. **ISO/PAS 21448:2022. Road vehicles — Safety of the intended functionality (SOTIF). ISO.**
    - Addresses perception system failures not caused by hardware faults; critical for AV and ADAS certification.

33. **Kagermann, H. et al. (2013). Recommendations for implementing the strategic initiative INDUSTRIE 4.0. *Acatech*.**
    - Seminal Industry 4.0 white paper that coined the term and defined the vision of smart manufacturing.

## Human-Robot Interaction and Ethics

34. **Lorenzini, M. et al. (2023). Ergonomic human-robot collaboration in industry: A review. *Frontiers in Robotics and AI*.**
    - Comprehensive review of ergonomics, safety, and usability in collaborative manufacturing with cobots.

35. **Villani, V. et al. (2018). Survey on human-robot collaboration in industrial settings. *Mechatronics*, 55, 248-266.**
    - Systematic survey of HRC challenges: safety, ergonomics, programming interfaces, acceptance.

## AI Ethics and Labor Economics

36. **Acemoglu, D. & Restrepo, P. (2022). Tasks, Automation, and the Rise in US Wage Inequality. *Econometrica*, 90(5), 1973-2016.**
    - Most rigorous empirical study of automation's labor market impact; 23.4% middle-skill job reduction quantification.

37. **Awad, E. et al. (2018). The Moral Machine experiment. *Nature*, 563, 59-64.**
    - 2.3M participants across 233 countries on AV ethical decision scenarios; reveals cross-cultural convergence and divergence.

38. **Buolamwini, J. & Gebru, T. (2018). Gender Shades: Intersectional Accuracy Disparities in Commercial Gender Classification. *FAccT 2018*.**
    - Demonstrated 10-34× higher error rates for dark-skinned women vs. light-skinned men in commercial facial recognition.

39. **Obermeyer, Z. et al. (2019). Dissecting racial bias in an algorithm used to manage the health of populations. *Science*, 366, 447-453.**
    - Exposed racial bias in Optum health risk algorithm; established gold-standard for algorithmic bias analysis.

40. **Lundberg, S.M. & Lee, S.I. (2017). A unified approach to interpreting model predictions. *NeurIPS 2017*.**
    - Introduced SHAP: the industry-standard feature attribution method for explaining ML model predictions.

## Agentic AI and LLM-Based Automation (2022-2026)

41. **Yao, S. et al. (2023). ReAct: Synergizing Reasoning and Acting in Language Models. *ICLR 2023*.**
    - Introduced ReAct: LLMs interleave reasoning traces and actions; foundation of modern agentic AI architectures.

42. **Ahn, M. et al. (2022). Do As I Can, Not As I Say: Grounding Language in Robotic Affordances. *arXiv:2204.01691*.**
    - SayCan: LLM high-level planning + robot affordance model execution; demonstrated on 551 real kitchen tasks.

43. **Mirhoseini, A. et al. (2021). A graph placement methodology for fast chip design. *Nature*, 594, 207-212.**
    - Google's RL chip floorplanning: 1000× faster than human experts; first RL deployment in semiconductor design.

44. **Xie, T. et al. (2024). OSWorld: Benchmarking Multimodal Agents for Open-Ended Tasks in Real Computer Environments. *arXiv:2404.07972*.**
    - OSWorld benchmark for computer-use agents; Claude 3.7 Sonnet achieves 38.1% (vs. 72% human).

    - 81% fewer injury-causing crashes than human drivers; 12M+ driverless miles data.

50. **EU (2024). Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 on Artificial Intelligence (EU AI Act). European Parliament.**
    - The world's first comprehensive AI regulation; risk-based framework; enforcement August 2026 for high-risk systems.
