# The History of Automation

**Version**: 3.0 | **Last Updated**: 2026-05-07  
**Scope**: An index of the comprehensive chronology of automation from ancient feedback mechanisms to AI-native systems.

---

The history of automation is not merely the history of replacing human labor with machines. It is the history of abstracting intelligence—from mechanical gears to electrical relays to silicon neural networks.

To explore the timeline, please select an era below:

## [Part 1: Antiquity to the Industrial Revolution (300 BCE – 1900 CE)](1.2.1_Antiquity_to_Industrial_Revolution.md)
*The mechanical origins of automation, early analog computers (like the Antikythera mechanism), programmable looms, and the birth of mathematical control theory.*

## [Part 2: The Electronic & Control Age (1900 – 1990)](1.2.2_The_Electronic_and_Control_Age.md)
*The formalization of PID control, the birth of Computer Numerical Control (CNC) machining, Programmable Logic Controllers (PLCs), and the deployment of the first industrial robots.*

## [Part 3: The Digital & AI Era (1990 – 2026)](1.2.3_The_Digital_and_AI_Era.md)
*The software standardization of automation, the DARPA Grand Challenges, the deep learning revolution in perception, and the rise of Agentic AI and Foundation Models.*
