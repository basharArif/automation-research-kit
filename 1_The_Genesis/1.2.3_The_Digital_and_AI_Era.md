# History of Automation: Part 3
## The Digital & AI Era (1990 – 2026)

**Scope**: The standardization of software, the deep learning revolution, modern autonomous vehicles, and the rise of Agentic AI.

---

## 1. Standardization and Connectivity (1990 - 2010)

### 1993: IEC 61131-3 Standard (PLC Programming)
**Event**: The International Electrotechnical Commission publishes the IEC 61131-3 standard.  
**Scope**: Unifies PLC programming into standard languages (Ladder logic, Structured text, Function block diagram).  
**Impact**: Drives global vendor interoperability and massively lowers the barrier to training new automation engineers.

### 2004–2005: The DARPA Grand Challenges
**Event**: DARPA hosts competitions challenging teams to build fully autonomous vehicles capable of navigating a 150-mile off-road course in the Mojave Desert.
**Significance**: In 2004, no vehicle completed the course. In 2005, five vehicles successfully finished (led by Stanford's "Stanley").
**Impact**: This catalyzed the modern autonomous vehicle (AV) industry, proving that LiDAR, computer vision, and probabilistic robotics could work outside controlled environments. It led directly to the founding of Waymo.

### 2005: OPC UA Standard Released
**Event**: Release of the Object Linking and Embedding (OLE) for Process Control Unified Architecture.  
**Purpose**: Standardize industrial data exchange with semantic meaning (not just raw values).  
**Impact**: Provides the critical communication backbone for Industry 4.0, bridging operational technology (OT) and information technology (IT).

### 2007: Release of ROS (Robot Operating System)
**Event**: Stanford AI Lab and Willow Garage release ROS.  
**Significance**: Provides a standard, open-source middleware for robotics (publish/subscribe messaging, hardware abstraction).  
**Impact**: Eliminates the need to "reinvent the wheel" for every new robot; democratizes robotics research globally.

## 2. The Deep Learning Revolution (2012 - 2020)

### 2012: AlexNet & The Deep Learning Boom
**Event**: A Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) called AlexNet destroys the competition in the ImageNet challenge, proving the superiority of deep learning over classical computer vision.
**Impact on Automation**: Transitioned robotic perception from brittle, hand-coded feature extraction to robust, learned representations. Allowed robots to "see" and classify objects in messy, unstructured environments like warehouses and homes.

### 2014: Emergence of RPA (Robotic Process Automation)
**Event**: Coining of the term RPA and rapid growth of companies like UiPath and Blue Prism.  
**Technology**: Software bots that mimic human UI interactions (clicks, typing) to automate repetitive clerical tasks.  
**Impact**: Automation aggressively expands beyond physical factories into the corporate back-office (finance, HR, data entry).

### 2016-2018: Deep Reinforcement Learning Successes
**Event**: DeepMind's AlphaGo defeats Lee Sedol (2016); OpenAI Five defeats human pros in Dota 2 (2018).  
**Automation Parallel**: OpenAI demonstrates a robot hand solving a Rubik's cube single-handed (2019) using Domain Randomization and reinforcement learning.  
**Significance**: Proves that AI can discover complex physical control policies entirely through simulation (Sim2Real transfer).

## 3. The Era of Foundation Models (2022 - 2026)

### 2023: Robotics Transformers (RT-1 & RT-2)
**Event**: Google DeepMind releases Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models.  
**Significance**: Instead of training a small model for one specific task, a massive LLM backbone is fine-tuned to output physical robot actions.  
**Impact**: Robots demonstrate "emergent reasoning"—e.g., asked to "pick up the extinct animal," the robot correctly identifies and picks up a plastic dinosaur without explicit programming.

### 2024: Open-Source Robotics Stacks & Octo
**Event**: Release of the Open X-Embodiment dataset and the open-source Octo model.  
**Significance**: 22 different robot types across 20 institutions share training data. A single model (Octo) is trained to control all of them.  
**Impact**: Proves that scaling laws apply to physical automation just as they do to language models.

### 2025: The Humanoid Pilot Phase
**Event**: Humanoid robots (Tesla Optimus, Figure 02, Agility Digit) transition from R&D labs to commercial pilot deployments in automotive factories and warehouses.  
**Significance**: The first viable general-purpose robotic form factor, capable of utilizing environments designed exclusively for humans.

### 2026: Agentic AI Replaces RPA
**Event**: The mainstream deployment of Computer-Use Agents (e.g., Anthropic Claude Computer Use, OpenAI Operator).  
**Significance**: Traditional deterministic RPA scripts are replaced by autonomous AI agents that perceive screens, reason about goals, and execute actions dynamically, recovering from UI changes automatically.
